How to Use RefBoard: A Complete Guide for Artists

Published on January 16, 2024 · 5 min read · Tutorial

As a digital artist, having a well-organized reference board can make the difference between a frustrating creative session and a productive one. RefBoard is designed specifically to solve this problem, providing artists with an intuitive, powerful tool for managing visual references. In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to master RefBoard.

Getting Started with RefBoard

1. Sign In and Create Your First Board

To begin using RefBoard, visit refboard.github.io and click the "Get Started" button. You'll be prompted to sign in with your Google account - this integration allows RefBoard to securely store your images in your personal Google Drive, giving you full control over your data.

Once signed in, you'll see your dashboard. Click "New Board" to create your first reference board. Give it a descriptive name - for example, "Character Design - Fantasy Warrior" or "Color Palette Research". Good naming helps you stay organized as your collection of boards grows.

2. Adding Images to Your Board

There are several ways to add reference images to your board:

  • Drag and Drop: The easiest method is to drag images directly from your file explorer or browser onto the canvas.
  • Upload Button: Click the upload button in the toolbar to select multiple files at once from your computer.
  • Paste from Clipboard: Copy an image (Ctrl+C) and paste it directly onto the canvas (Ctrl+V).

RefBoard supports all major image formats including JPG, PNG, GIF, and even animated WebP files. You can also add videos in MP4 or WebM format, which is perfect for studying motion and animation references.

Organizing Your References

The Infinite Canvas

Unlike traditional mood board tools that force you into a grid layout, RefBoard gives you an infinite canvas to work with. This means you can spread out your references spatially in whatever way makes sense for your project. Many artists organize references by:

  • Topic or theme (characters, environments, props)
  • Stage of development (inspiration, rough studies, final references)
  • Color schemes or visual styles
  • Chronological progression of ideas

Navigation and Zoom

Navigating your board is intuitive and smooth. Hold the Space bar and drag with your mouse to pan around the canvas. Use your mouse wheel to zoom in and out - RefBoard maintains crisp image quality at all zoom levels, from a bird's-eye view of your entire board down to pixel-level detail.

Pro Tip: Use the minimap in the bottom-right corner to quickly navigate to different areas of large boards. Click anywhere on the minimap to instantly jump to that location.

Grouping Related Images

One of RefBoard's most powerful features is the ability to group related images together. To create a group:

  1. Select multiple images by clicking and dragging a selection box around them
  2. Press Ctrl+G (Cmd+G on Mac) or click the "Group" button
  3. Give your group a name like "Armor References" or "Lighting Studies"

Groups can be moved, resized, and even nested within other groups. This hierarchical organization allows you to build complex reference collections while keeping everything manageable. Groups also have colored borders that you can customize to visually distinguish different categories at a glance.

Advanced Features

Collaboration and Sharing

RefBoard shines when working with teams. Click the Share button to invite collaborators by email. You can grant view-only access for clients or stakeholders, or edit access for team members who need to add their own references. Changes sync in real-time, so everyone sees updates immediately.

Export and Presentation

When you're ready to present your board or use it in another application, RefBoard offers several export options. You can export your entire board as a high-resolution PNG image, perfect for presentation slides or printing. Individual images can be downloaded by right-clicking on them, and you can export board data as JSON for backup purposes.

Search and Filter

As your boards grow to hundreds of images, finding specific references becomes crucial. RefBoard's search feature lets you filter images by filename, group name, or tags you've added. This makes it easy to quickly locate that one perfect reference you remember adding weeks ago.

Best Practices for Artists

1. Start Broad, Then Focus

Begin your reference collection by gathering a wide variety of images related to your project. Don't worry about perfect organization at first - just get everything onto the canvas. Once you have a good collection, start organizing into groups and removing less relevant references.

2. Use Multiple Boards

Instead of cramming everything into one massive board, create separate boards for different aspects of your project. For a character design project, you might have:

  • A board for overall character inspiration and mood
  • A board specifically for costume and armor references
  • A board for color palette exploration
  • A board for pose and anatomy studies

3. Regular Cleanup

As your project evolves, some references become less relevant. Regularly review your board and remove or archive outdated references. This keeps your board focused and prevents overwhelming visual clutter that can slow down your creative decision-making.

Conclusion

RefBoard transforms the way digital artists work with reference images. By providing an infinite canvas, powerful organization tools, and seamless collaboration features, it eliminates the frustration of managing references across multiple tools and folders. Whether you're a character designer, concept artist, illustrator, or any other creative professional, RefBoard adapts to your workflow.

The key to mastering RefBoard is to start simple and gradually explore its features as your needs grow. Begin with basic image placement and navigation, then experiment with grouping, sharing, and advanced organization as you become more comfortable. Most importantly, make RefBoard a central part of your creative process - the more you use it, the more efficient and organized your workflow becomes.

Ready to revolutionize your reference workflow? Start using RefBoard today and experience the difference a purpose-built reference tool can make.
