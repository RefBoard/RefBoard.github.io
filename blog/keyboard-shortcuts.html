Master RefBoard: Essential Keyboard Shortcuts

Published on January 15, 2024 · 4 min read · Tutorial

Keyboard shortcuts are the secret weapon of productive artists. While RefBoard is designed to be intuitive with mouse and touchpad controls, mastering keyboard shortcuts can speed up your workflow by up to 300%. In this guide, we'll cover all the essential shortcuts that will transform you from a RefBoard beginner to a power user.

Navigation & View Controls

The m ost frequently used shortcuts in RefBoard are for navigating your canvas. These shortcuts let you move around quickly without constantly reaching for UI buttons.

Shortcut Action Description
Space + Drag Pan Canvas Hold Space and drag to move around your board
Mouse Wheel Zoom In/Out Scroll to zoom in and out of your canvas
Ctrl + 0 Fit to Screen Reset zoom to fit all content on screen
Ctrl + + Zoom In Zoom in to your canvas
Ctrl + - Zoom Out Zoom out from your canvas

Selection & Editing

These shortcuts help you quickly select, modify, and organize items on your board. They're essential for maintaining a clean, well-organized reference collection.

Ctrl + A Select All Select all items on the canvas
Ctrl + Click Multi-Select Add or remove items from selection
Shift + Drag Add to Selection Draw selection box to add to current selection
Delete Delete Item Remove selected items from the board
Ctrl + D Duplicate Create a copy of selected items

Organization Shortcuts

Keeping your references organized is crucial for large projects. These shortcuts make grouping and arranging items fast and efficient.

Ctrl + G Group Selection Create a group from selected items
Ctrl + Shift + G Ungroup Break apart a group into individual items
Ctrl + ] Bring Forward Move item up one layer
Ctrl + [ Send Backward Move item down one layer

File Operations

These shortcuts speed up common file operations, letting you add and manage content without breaking your creative flow.

Ctrl + V Paste Image Paste image from clipboard onto canvas
Ctrl + C Copy Copy selected items
Ctrl + Z Undo Undo last action
Ctrl + Y Redo Redo last undone action
Ctrl + S Save Manually save your board (auto-save is always on)

Pro Tips for Maximum Productivity

1. Use Two-Hand Navigation

Keep your left hand on the keyboard for shortcuts while your right hand controls the mouse. This "keyboard-mouse combo" technique is how professional artists achieve maximum speed. Practice holding Space with your thumb while dragging with the mouse to pan effortlessly around large boards.

2. Chain Shortcuts Together

Many workflows involve multiple shortcuts in sequence. For example, a common pattern when organizing references is: Select multiple items (Shift + Drag), Group them (Ctrl + G), then Duplicate the group (Ctrl + D). Practice these chains until they become muscle memory.

3. Customize Your Workflow

While RefBoard doesn't currently support custom keyboard shortcuts, you can optimize your workflow by arranging frequently-accessed items in easy-to-reach screen positions. Place your most-used groups near the center of your screen, and use the minimap for quick jumps to other areas.

Mac vs Windows Differences

Mac users should substitute Cmd for Ctrl in most shortcuts. The only exceptions are selection shortcuts, which work identically on both platforms. For example, Ctrl + G on Windows becomes Cmd + G on Mac.

Shortcuts Cheat Sheet

Here's a quick reference guide you can bookmark or print out:

  • Pan: Space + Drag
  • Zoom: Mouse Wheel
  • Select All: Ctrl + A
  • Group: Ctrl + G
  • Ungroup: Ctrl + Shift + G
  • Delete: Del
  • Duplicate: Ctrl + D
  • Undo: Ctrl + Z
  • Redo: Ctrl + Y
  • Paste: Ctrl + V

Practice Makes Perfect

Like any skill, mastering keyboard shortcuts takes practice. Start by incorporating just 2-3 shortcuts into your daily workflow. Once those become natural, add a few more. Within a week, you'll be navigating and organizing references at lightning speed, and RefBoard will feel like a natural extension of your creative process.

The time investment in learning these shortcuts pays dividends quickly. What used to take dozens of mouse clicks now happens with a single keystroke, freeing your mind to focus on what really matters: your creative work.
