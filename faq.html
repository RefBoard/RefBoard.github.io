What is RefBoard?
RefBoard is a powerful reference board tool designed specifically for artists, designers, and creative professionals. It allows you to organize, manage, and view reference images in a customizable workspace. Unlike traditional mood boards, RefBoard offers advanced features like infinite canvas, zoom controls, grouping, and real-time collaboration, making it the perfect tool for character design, concept art, and creative projects.
Is RefBoard free to use?
Yes! RefBoard is completely free to use. You can access the web version at refboard.github.io or download the desktop application for Windows. All core features including unlimited boards, image uploads, and Google Drive integration are available at no cost. We believe in providing powerful tools to the creative community without barriers.
How do I create my first board?
Creating your first board is simple! After signing in with your Google account, click the "New Board" button on the dashboard. Give your board a name (like "Character Design" or "Color Palette"), and you'll be taken to an infinite canvas where you can start adding reference images. You can drag and drop images directly onto the canvas, or use the upload button to select multiple files at once.

üé® Features & Usage

What file formats does RefBoard support?
RefBoard supports all major image and video formats. For images, you can upload JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP, and SVG files. For videos, we support MP4, WebM, and MOV formats. There's no file size limit for images, though we recommend keeping individual files under 50MB for optimal performance. All files are stored securely in your Google Drive.
How do I organize images on my board?
RefBoard offers multiple ways to organize your references. You can freely position images anywhere on the infinite canvas by dragging them. Use the grouping feature to create collections of related images - simply select multiple items and press Ctrl+G (Cmd+G on Mac). You can resize images individually, rotate them, and even create nested groups. The search and filter features help you quickly find specific references in larger boards.
Can I collaborate with others on a board?
Yes! RefBoard supports real-time collaboration. To share a board, click the "Share" button in the top-right corner and add collaborators by their email addresses. You can set permissions to view-only or edit access. Multiple users can work on the same board simultaneously, with changes syncing in real-time. This makes RefBoard perfect for team projects, art direction, and remote collaboration.
What are the keyboard shortcuts?
RefBoard has many keyboard shortcuts for efficient workflow: Space+Drag to pan the canvas, Mouse Wheel to zoom, Ctrl+Z/Y for undo/redo, Ctrl+G to group selected items, Delete to remove items, and Ctrl+A to select all. Hold Shift while resizing to maintain aspect ratio. Press Ctrl+D to duplicate selected items. For a complete list, visit our keyboard shortcuts guide in the documentation.

üíæ Storage & Data

Where are my images stored?
All your images are stored securely in your personal Google Drive account. When you upload an image to RefBoard, it's automatically saved to a dedicated "RefBoard" folder in your Drive. This means you have full control over your data, and your images don't take up space on our servers. You can access these files directly from Google Drive at any time, and they count toward your Google Drive storage quota.
Is my data safe and private?
Absolutely. RefBoard takes privacy seriously. We use Google's secure OAuth authentication and only request the minimum necessary permissions. Your board data is stored in your own Google Drive account, encrypted in transit and at rest. We never sell or share your data with third parties. You can revoke RefBoard's access to your Google account at any time through your Google Account settings. For more details, please review our Privacy Policy.
Can I export my boards?
Yes! You can export your boards in multiple formats. Use the "Export" menu to save your entire board as a high-resolution PNG image, perfect for presentations or printing. You can also export board data as JSON for backup purposes or to transfer boards between accounts. Individual images can be downloaded directly from the board by right-clicking on them.

What browsers are supported?
RefBoard works best on modern browsers. We officially support the latest versions of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, and Safari. For the best experience, we recommend using Chrome or Edge, as they offer the best performance for our canvas rendering engine. The desktop application (built with Electron) provides native performance on Windows.
I'm experiencing slow performance, what can I do?
If RefBoard feels slow, try these solutions: First, close other browser tabs to free up memory. Second, reduce the number of high-resolution images on a single board - consider creating multiple boards for large projects. Third, use the desktop application instead of the web version for better performance. Fourth, enable hardware acceleration in your browser settings. If issues persist, contact our support team with details about your system and browser.
How do I report a bug or request a feature?
We love hearing from our users! To report bugs or request features, visit our GitHub repository and create an issue, or contact us directly through the contact form on this website. When reporting bugs, please include your browser version, operating system, and steps to reproduce the issue. For feature requests, describe your use case and how the feature would improve your workflow. We review all feedback and prioritize based on community needs.
